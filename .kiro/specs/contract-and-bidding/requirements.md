# Requirements Document

## Introduction

This document covers the **Contract & Bidding** feature of the Effix B2B CRM & Billing platform. The platform serves companies that can simultaneously act as buyers (clients) and sellers (vendors), enabling them to post procurement requests (RFPs), receive and negotiate bids, generate purchase orders (Bon de Commande), create contracts, manage work execution, and track everything on a shared operational calendar.

A significant portion of the frontend UI already exists as mock-data-driven components (`WorkflowsView`, `WorkBoard`, `CalendarView`, `MessagesView`, `TeamsView`). The backend has a `BusinessWorkflow` state machine, `Devis` (bid/quote), `BonDeCommande`, `Facture`, `ProjectTask`, and `ProjectMilestone` entities. The goal of this feature is to **close the gaps** between the existing frontend mock data and the real API, and to add the missing entities and endpoints that the full user journey requires.

---

## Glossary

- **RFP (Request for Proposal)**: A public or private procurement request posted by a buyer company to solicit bids from vendors. Represented by `BusinessWorkflow` in the backend.
- **Bid / Devis**: A formal offer submitted by a vendor company in response to an RFP. Represented by the `Devis` entity.
- **Negotiation**: An iterative exchange of messages between the buyer and a specific vendor, attached to a bid, to agree on terms before acceptance.
- **BC (Bon de Commande)**: A purchase order generated by the system when the buyer accepts a bid. The vendor must confirm the BC before the contract is formally created.
- **Contract**: The formal agreement created after the vendor confirms the BC. Derived from the accepted `BusinessWorkflow` + `BonDeCommande`. Not a separate entity today — needs to be made explicit.
- **Work Item**: A discrete unit of work within a contract. Can be of type `material`, `service`, or `subscription`. Currently missing as a dedicated entity; only `ProjectTask` and `ProjectMilestone` exist.
- **Task**: A granular action item within a Work Item, assigned to a specific user, with a start date and duration in days.
- **Team**: A named group of users that can be assigned to a Work Item. Currently frontend-only mock data.
- **Workflow Status**: The state machine enum: `DRAFT → PUBLISHED → EVALUATION → IN_PROGRESS → UNDER_TEST → FULLY_INVOICED`.
- **Bid Status**: The negotiation lifecycle of a single bid: `SUBMITTED → NEGOTIATING → BC_PENDING_VENDOR → CONTRACT_ESTABLISHED → REJECTED`.
- **Work Type**: Enum classifying the nature of a Work Item: `material` (physical goods delivery), `service` (one-time professional service), `subscription` (recurring monthly service).
- **Holiday**: A non-working day that must be excluded when scheduling service and subscription Work Items.
- **Buyer Company**: The company that posted the RFP and will receive the deliverable.
- **Seller Company**: The company that submitted the winning bid and will deliver the work.
- **Platform_Admin**: A system-level administrator with cross-company access.
- **Company_Admin**: An administrator scoped to a single company.
- **Company_User**: A standard user scoped to a single company.
- **Marketplace**: The public listing of all `PUBLISHED` RFPs visible to all authenticated companies.
- **Negotiation_Message**: A message tied to a specific bid (not a general company-to-company message), used to exchange counter-proposals during bid negotiation.
- **Payment_Schedule**: A list of recurring payment installments generated for `subscription` Work Items.
- **Calendar_Event**: A date-bound entry on the operational calendar derived from Work Item dates, Task dates, or Contract milestones.

## Requirements

### Requirement 1: RFP Creation and Publication

**User Story:** As a Company_Admin, I want to create a detailed RFP and publish it to the marketplace, so that vendor companies can discover and bid on my procurement needs.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE RFP_Creator SHALL accept the following fields when creating an RFP: `title`, `description`, `budgetCeiling`, `technicalCategory`, `submissionDeadline`, `expectedDeliveryDate`, `currency`, `region`, `requirements` (list of strings), `constraints` (list of strings), `conditions` (text), `evaluationCriteria` (text), and `rfpFileUrl` (optional attachment URL).
2. THE RFP_Creator SHALL also accept a `workflowType` field (required, enum: `material`, `service`, `subscription`) that determines the contract execution behavior (delivery calendar events, service scheduling, or recurring billing respectively); IF `workflowType` is absent or not one of the three allowed values, THEN THE System SHALL return HTTP 400 Bad Request.
3. WHEN an RFP is created, THE System SHALL persist it with `stateCode = DRAFT` and associate it with the authenticated user's company as `buyerCompany`.
4. WHEN a Company_Admin publishes an RFP, THE System SHALL transition its `stateCode` from `DRAFT` to `PUBLISHED` and make it visible in the Marketplace.
5. IF a user attempts to publish an RFP that does not belong to their company, THEN THE System SHALL return HTTP 403 Forbidden.
6. WHEN an RFP is published, THE System SHALL send a notification to the buyer company confirming publication.
7. THE Marketplace_API SHALL expose a `GET /workflows/active` endpoint that returns all `PUBLISHED` RFPs with their full detail including `requirements`, `constraints`, `conditions`, `evaluationCriteria`, `region`, and `attachedFiles`.
8. THE Marketplace_API SHALL support filtering active RFPs by `technicalCategory`, `budgetCeiling` (maximum), `region`, and `createdAt` (posted after date).

### Requirement 2: Bid Submission

**User Story:** As a Company_Admin or Company_User acting as a vendor, I want to submit a bid on a published RFP, so that I can compete for the contract.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a vendor submits a bid on a `PUBLISHED` RFP, THE System SHALL create a `Devis` record linked to the workflow and the vendor's company, and transition the workflow to `EVALUATION` state.
2. THE Bid_Submission_API SHALL accept: `referenceNumber`, `issueDate`, `validUntil`, `deliveryLeadTime` (days), `downPaymentPercentage`, `balanceDueDays`, `vendorNotes`, `technicalProposalFileUrl`, `financialQuoteFileUrl`, and `items` (array of `{ articleId, quantity, discountAmount }`).
3. IF a company attempts to submit a bid on its own RFP, THEN THE System SHALL return HTTP 400 Bad Request with a descriptive error.
4. IF a company submits more than one bid on the same RFP, THEN THE System SHALL create a new `Devis` with an incremented `revisionNumber` rather than replacing the existing bid.
5. WHEN a bid is submitted, THE System SHALL calculate and persist `subTotalHT`, `totalTVA`, and `totalTTC` using the `InvoiceCalculatorService`.
6. WHEN a bid is submitted, THE System SHALL notify the buyer company that a new bid has been received.

### Requirement 3: Bid Negotiation via Messages

**User Story:** As a buyer or vendor, I want to exchange negotiation messages tied to a specific bid, so that we can agree on terms without losing context.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Negotiation_Message entity SHALL have the following fields: `id`, `bidId` (foreign key to `Devis`), `workflowId` (foreign key to `BusinessWorkflow`), `senderCompanyId`, `senderUserId`, `content` (text), `createdAt`, and `messageType` (enum: `BUYER_MESSAGE`, `VENDOR_MESSAGE`, `SYSTEM_NOTE`).
2. WHEN a buyer sends a negotiation message on a bid, THE System SHALL persist the message with `messageType = BUYER_MESSAGE` and update the bid's status to `NEGOTIATING`.
3. WHEN a vendor sends a negotiation message on a bid, THE System SHALL persist the message with `messageType = VENDOR_MESSAGE`.
4. THE Negotiation_API SHALL expose `POST /workflows/:id/bids/:bidId/messages` to send a negotiation message and `GET /workflows/:id/bids/:bidId/messages` to retrieve the full thread for a bid.
5. IF a user from a company not involved in the bid (neither buyer nor the bidding vendor) attempts to read or post negotiation messages, THEN THE System SHALL return HTTP 403 Forbidden.
6. WHEN a negotiation message is posted, THE System SHALL send a notification to the other party (buyer notifies vendor, vendor notifies buyer).
7. THE Negotiation_Message entity SHALL be distinct from the general `Message` entity (which handles company-to-company chat) so that bid context is preserved independently.

### Requirement 4: Bid Acceptance and BC Generation

**User Story:** As a buyer Company_Admin, I want to accept a bid and have the system automatically generate a Bon de Commande (BC), so that the vendor can confirm the purchase order before work begins.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a buyer accepts a bid, THE System SHALL mark the accepted `Devis` as `isAccepted = true`, set all other bids on the same RFP to `isRejected = true` with a system-generated rejection note, and transition the workflow to `IN_PROGRESS`.
2. WHEN a bid is accepted, THE System SHALL automatically generate a `BonDeCommande` record linked to the workflow, the accepted `Devis`, the buyer company, and the seller company, with a system-generated `clientPoReferenceNumber` following the pattern `BC-{YEAR}-{workflowId}-{sequence}`. THE `BonDeCommande` SHALL store a direct foreign-key reference (`acceptedDevisId`) to the accepted `Devis` record so that the purchase order can always be traced back to the originating bid.
3. THE `BonDeCommande` SHALL copy the following fields from the accepted `Devis`: `items`, `subTotalHT`, `totalTVA`, `totalTTC`, `deliveryLeadTime`, `downPaymentPercentage`, `balanceDueDays`, and `shippingAddress` (if provided).
4. WHEN a BC is generated, THE System SHALL set the bid's status to `BC_PENDING_VENDOR` and notify the seller company that a BC is awaiting their confirmation.
5. IF a material-type workflow bid is accepted and the seller's inventory has insufficient stock for any item, THEN THE System SHALL return HTTP 400 Bad Request and not accept the bid.
6. THE Devis entity SHALL include a `status` field (enum: `SUBMITTED`, `NEGOTIATING`, `BC_PENDING_VENDOR`, `CONTRACT_ESTABLISHED`, `REJECTED`) to track the bid lifecycle independently of the workflow state.

### Requirement 5: Vendor BC Confirmation and Contract Creation

**User Story:** As a vendor Company_Admin, I want to confirm the Bon de Commande, so that the contract is formally established and work can begin.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Contract_API SHALL expose `POST /workflows/:id/bc/confirm` which allows the seller company to confirm the BC.
2. WHEN the seller confirms the BC, THE System SHALL update the `BonDeCommande` with `signatureTimestamp = now()` and transition the bid status to `CONTRACT_ESTABLISHED`.
3. WHEN the BC is confirmed, THE System SHALL set `contractStartDate = now()` and `contractStatus = 'ACTIVE'` on the `BusinessWorkflow` record; these updates SHALL be committed atomically in the same transaction as the `BonDeCommande` signature and bid status transition.
4. THE `BusinessWorkflow` entity SHALL include a `workflowType` field (enum: `material`, `service`, `subscription`) that is set at RFP creation time and used to determine contract behavior.
5. WHEN a contract is established, THE System SHALL notify both the buyer and seller companies.
6. IF a user from a company not involved in the workflow attempts to confirm the BC, THEN THE System SHALL return HTTP 403 Forbidden.

### Requirement 6: Work Item Management

**User Story:** As a Company_Admin (seller), I want to create and manage Work Items within a contract, so that the execution of the contract is tracked at a granular level.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE System SHALL introduce a `WorkItem` entity with fields: `id`, `workflowId` (FK to `BusinessWorkflow`), `title`, `description`, `type` (enum: `material`, `service`, `subscription`), `status` (enum: `PENDING`, `IN_PROGRESS`, `COMPLETED`), `assignedTeamId` (FK to `Team`), `startDate`, `endDate`, `monthlyAmount` (decimal, only applicable for `subscription` type), `createdAt`.
2. THE Work_API SHALL expose `POST /workflows/:id/works` to create a Work Item under a contract, `GET /workflows/:id/works` to list all Work Items for a contract, `GET /works/:workId` to retrieve a single Work Item with its tasks, and `PATCH /works/:workId` to update a Work Item.
3. WHEN a Work Item is created, THE System SHALL validate that the parent workflow is in `IN_PROGRESS` state; IF NOT, THEN THE System SHALL return HTTP 400 Bad Request.
4. THE Work_API SHALL expose `POST /works/:workId/tasks` to add a task to a Work Item, `PATCH /works/:workId/tasks/:taskId` to update a task (status, assignee, date, durationDays, description), and `DELETE /works/:workId/tasks/:taskId` to remove a task.
5. WHEN all tasks within a Work Item are in `completed` status, THE System SHALL automatically update the Work Item's `status` to `COMPLETED`.
6. THE `WorkItem` status SHALL be derived from its tasks: if no tasks exist or all are `todo`, status is `PENDING`; if any task is `in-progress` or a mix of completed and non-completed, status is `IN_PROGRESS`; if all tasks are `completed`, status is `COMPLETED`.

### Requirement 7: Team Entity and Assignment to Work Items

**User Story:** As a Company_Admin, I want to create teams and assign them to Work Items, so that the right people are responsible for each piece of work.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE System SHALL introduce a `Team` entity with fields: `id`, `name`, `description`, `managerId` (FK to `User`), `companyId` (FK to `Company`), `tags` (array of strings), `createdAt`.
2. THE Team_API SHALL expose `POST /teams` to create a team, `GET /teams` to list all teams for the authenticated company, `GET /teams/:id` to retrieve a team with its members, `PATCH /teams/:id` to update team details, and `POST /teams/:id/members` / `DELETE /teams/:id/members/:userId` to manage membership.
3. WHEN a Work Item is created or updated with an `assignedTeamId`, THE System SHALL validate that the team belongs to the same company as the seller on the contract; IF NOT, THEN THE System SHALL return HTTP 400 Bad Request.
4. THE `GET /teams/:id` response SHALL include the list of team members (user id, email, role) and the list of Work Items currently assigned to the team.
5. WHERE a company has no teams, THE System SHALL allow Work Items to be created without an `assignedTeamId` (the field is optional).

### Requirement 8: Calendar Integration — Material Work Items

**User Story:** As a user, I want material delivery Work Items to appear on the operational calendar with packaging, departure, and arrival dates, so that logistics are visible at a glance.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a `material` Work Item is created, THE System SHALL derive three `CalendarEvent` records and persist them to the `calendar_events` table: a `PACKAGING` event on `startDate`, a `DEPARTURE` event on `startDate + 1 business day`, and an `ARRIVAL` event on `endDate`.
2. THE Calendar_API SHALL expose `GET /calendar/events?from=YYYY-MM-DD&to=YYYY-MM-DD` which reads from the `calendar_events` table and returns all calendar events for the authenticated company within the date range, including events where the company is the buyer (receiving deliveries) and events where the company is the seller (sending deliveries).
3. WHEN the company is the seller on two or more material Work Items with overlapping dates, THE System SHALL flag a scheduling conflict in the calendar event response with `hasConflict: true` and a list of conflicting Work Item IDs.
4. WHEN the company is the buyer receiving two or more material deliveries on the same day, THE System SHALL NOT flag a conflict — both events SHALL appear independently on that day.
5. WHEN a user clicks a calendar event, THE Calendar_API SHALL return the full detail of the associated Work Item and a link to the parent contract (`contractId`, `contractTitle`).

### Requirement 9: Calendar Integration — Service and Subscription Work Items

**User Story:** As a user, I want service and subscription Work Items to occupy a date range on the calendar while avoiding holidays, so that scheduling is realistic and conflicts are visible.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a `service` or `subscription` Work Item is created with a `startDate` and `endDate`, THE System SHALL generate `CalendarEvent` records of type `SERVICE_DAY` (for `service` type) or `BILLING_CYCLE` (for `subscription` type) and persist them to the `calendar_events` table, covering all business days between `startDate` and `endDate` (inclusive) and excluding any dates listed in the company's holiday calendar.
2. THE System SHALL expose `POST /holidays` and `GET /holidays` endpoints to allow Company_Admins to manage their company's holiday list (date + label).
3. WHEN a `service` Work Item is scheduled and the seller company already has another `service` Work Item occupying any of the same business days, THE System SHALL flag a scheduling conflict with `hasConflict: true` on the affected calendar events.
4. WHEN the company is the buyer receiving a service on days that overlap with another incoming service, THE System SHALL NOT flag a conflict — both events SHALL appear on the calendar independently.
5. WHEN a `subscription` Work Item is created, THE System SHALL generate recurring monthly calendar events from `startDate` for the duration of the subscription, each representing a billing cycle.

### Requirement 10: Subscription Payment Schedule

**User Story:** As a buyer or seller, I want subscription Work Items to have an auto-generated payment schedule, so that recurring billing is tracked and predictable.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a `subscription` Work Item is created with a `startDate`, `endDate`, and `monthlyAmount`, THE System SHALL generate a `PaymentSchedule` with one `PaymentInstallment` per calendar month between `startDate` and `endDate`.
2. EACH `PaymentInstallment` SHALL have: `id`, `workItemId`, `dueDate` (first business day of the month), `amount`, `status` (enum: `PENDING`, `PAID`, `OVERDUE`), `paidAt` (nullable).
3. THE Payment_Schedule_API SHALL expose `GET /works/:workId/payment-schedule` to retrieve all installments for a subscription Work Item.
4. THE Payment_Schedule_API SHALL expose `PATCH /works/:workId/payment-schedule/:installmentId` to mark an installment as `PAID` with a `paidAt` timestamp.
5. WHEN the current date passes a `PaymentInstallment`'s `dueDate` and the status is still `PENDING`, THE System SHALL update the status to `OVERDUE` (via a scheduled job or on-read computation).

4. WHEN a buyer negotiates a bid by sending a message, THE System SHALL update the bid's `status` to `NEGOTIATING` if it was previously `SUBMITTED`.
5. THE RFP_API SHALL expose `GET /workflows/:id/bids` to list all bids on a specific workflow, accessible only to the buyer company and Platform_Admin.

4. WHEN a vendor views their bids, THE System SHALL group them by status: `SUBMITTED`, `NEGOTIATING`, `BC_PENDING_VENDOR`, `CONTRACT_ESTABLISHED`, `REJECTED`.

### Requirement 17: Data Integrity and State Machine Enforcement

**User Story:** As a platform operator, I want the system to enforce valid state transitions and prevent data corruption, so that the contract lifecycle is always consistent.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE System SHALL enforce that bids can only be submitted on workflows in `PUBLISHED` or `EVALUATION` state; IF the workflow is in any other state, THEN THE System SHALL return HTTP 400 Bad Request.
2. THE System SHALL enforce that bid acceptance can only occur on workflows in `EVALUATION` state; IF NOT, THEN THE System SHALL return HTTP 400 Bad Request.
3. THE System SHALL enforce that Work Items can only be created on workflows in `IN_PROGRESS` state; IF NOT, THEN THE System SHALL return HTTP 400 Bad Request.
4. THE System SHALL enforce that a BC can only be confirmed by the seller company on the workflow; IF a different company attempts confirmation, THEN THE System SHALL return HTTP 403 Forbidden.
5. THE System SHALL enforce that a workflow cannot transition backwards in the state machine (e.g., from `IN_PROGRESS` back to `PUBLISHED`); IF such a transition is attempted, THEN THE System SHALL return HTTP 400 Bad Request.
6. WHEN a bid is rejected, THE System SHALL set `isRejected = true` on the `Devis` and record a `WorkflowHistory` entry with the rejection reason.

### Requirement 18: Frontend API Integration

**User Story:** As a developer, I want the existing frontend components to replace their mock data with real API calls, so that the UI reflects live backend state.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE `WorkflowsView` component SHALL fetch RFPs, bids, and contracts from the API on mount, replacing all static mock data imports; WHEN the API call is in flight, THE component SHALL display a loading indicator, and IF the call fails, THE component SHALL display an error state.
2. THE `WorkBoard` component SHALL persist all task mutations (create, update, delete, add comment) to the API in real time; WHEN a task is moved between columns, THE component SHALL call `PATCH /works/:workId/tasks/:taskId` with the new status before updating local state.
3. THE `CalendarView` component SHALL fetch events from `GET /calendar/events` (with `from` and `to` query parameters derived from the currently displayed date range) instead of importing the static `INITIAL_CONTRACTS` mock; WHEN the displayed month changes, THE component SHALL re-fetch events for the new range.
4. THE `TeamsView` component SHALL fetch teams via `GET /teams` on mount and SHALL persist team mutations (create team, update team, add member, remove member) via the corresponding `/teams` endpoints instead of mutating local state only.
5. WHEN any frontend component receives an HTTP 401 response from the API, THE component SHALL redirect the user to the login page.
6. WHEN any frontend component receives an HTTP 403 response from the API, THE component SHALL display a permission-denied message without crashing.

## Correctness Properties

The following properties are suitable for property-based testing. They describe invariants and round-trip behaviors that must hold across all valid inputs.

### Property 1: Bid Total Calculation Invariant

FOR ALL valid bid item arrays and a `globalDiscountRate` in [0, 100), THE `InvoiceCalculatorService.applyTotals()` SHALL produce a `totalTTC` equal to `subTotalHT + totalTVA`, where `subTotalHT = Σ max(0, unitPriceHT × quantity − discountAmount) × (1 − globalDiscountRate/100)` and `totalTVA = Σ lineHT × tvaRate/100 × (1 − globalDiscountRate/100)`. This invariant must hold regardless of the number of items, quantities, or discount values.

### Property 2: Work Item Status Derivation Invariant

FOR ALL Work Items with a non-empty task list, THE derived `status` SHALL satisfy: if all tasks have `status = completed` then `workItemStatus = COMPLETED`; if any task has `status = in-progress` or the set contains a mix of `completed` and non-`completed` tasks then `workItemStatus = IN_PROGRESS`; if all tasks have `status = todo` then `workItemStatus = PENDING`. This invariant must hold after every task status update.

### Property 3: Payment Schedule Completeness

FOR ALL subscription Work Items with a valid `startDate`, `endDate` (where `endDate > startDate`), and positive `monthlyAmount`, THE generated `PaymentSchedule` SHALL contain exactly `ceil(months_between(startDate, endDate))` installments, and no installment `dueDate` SHALL fall on a Saturday (day 6) or Sunday (day 0).

### Property 4: Calendar Event Non-Overlap for Seller

FOR ALL pairs of `service` or `material` Work Items assigned to the same seller company where the date ranges overlap, THE calendar event response SHALL include `hasConflict: true` on both events. Conversely, FOR ALL pairs of Work Items where the date ranges do not overlap, `hasConflict` SHALL be `false`.

### Property 5: Negotiation Message Thread Ordering

FOR ALL negotiation message threads on a bid, THE `GET /workflows/:id/bids/:bidId/messages` endpoint SHALL return messages ordered by `createdAt` ascending, and the count of returned messages SHALL equal the total number of messages persisted for that bid. No message from a different bid SHALL appear in the thread.

### Property 6: State Machine Monotonicity

FOR ALL valid sequences of state transitions applied to a `BusinessWorkflow`, the `stateCode` SHALL only advance forward in the defined order (`DRAFT → PUBLISHED → EVALUATION → IN_PROGRESS → UNDER_TEST → FULLY_INVOICED`), with the single exception of the `rejectTest` transition which moves `UNDER_TEST → IN_PROGRESS`. Any attempt to apply a transition that would move the state backward (other than the defined exception) SHALL result in an error and leave the `stateCode` unchanged.

### Property 7: BC Reference Number Uniqueness

FOR ALL `BonDeCommande` records created by the system, the `clientPoReferenceNumber` SHALL be unique across the entire database. Generating two BCs for two different accepted bids SHALL never produce the same reference number, regardless of the order of concurrent creation.
